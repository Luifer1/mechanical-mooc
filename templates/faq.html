How it works.

How do I sign up?

Visit our PWYM landing page and sign up any time. If you like, you are free to join the PWYM Community so we can send you regular updates about courses and activities. Once there, you can just follow along with the announcements we will be posting in the discussion forum. The whole idea of sign-up is so 2013. This is not a really a course, it's a ... community.

How different will PWYM 2 be from PWYM 1

Many of the core projects and experiences will be the same, however, we will be focusing on creative music and audio production skills using the music of Peter Gabriel. In addition to assisting you in analyzing and making your own music, we'll augment the class with interviews from musicians who've performed and collaborated with Peter in the past. So we believe experienced PWYM participants will have a chance to make new connections and go deeper--and help others along the way.

In the future, we also plan on adding additional artist material and learning modules, in partnership with recording artists, chamber musicians and composers and film scorers. We have grand plans for the content within the PWYM Community. We also want to hear from you about what content you'd like to see us curate and create for you. Share your ideas in the PWYM Community forum.

Why are you shifting to a new discussion platform?

Our G+ communities were great, but the main thing we're hoping to gain is to have more continuity of discussions. Key questions and discussions in last year's G+ community became buried, so we're hoping they will be more visible when entering the forum. Also, we're hoping to reduce advertising and spam. (We realize there are many positive aspects to G+ too, so it will be an experiment.)

What time are the live seminars? What if that time doesn't work for me?

Taking into account what we learned in PWYM 1.0, we are approaching the live interview sessions differently from now on. When we do live events, we will host the conversations on the Unhangout platform. We will send reminders by email at least a week in advance and also post announcements in the discussion forum. You can watch and discuss live, or watch the recording at a more convenient time. The only downside is that you would miss the live discussion. We'll ask you to post questions before the live events so that we draw upon those when interviewing our guests.

I have a question about how this works - Where can I get help?

The best place is our discussion forum. The forum will be the central place for conversations with other participants, to share your work with others, and give feedback.

How much time will the course take?

We don't require a particular commitment - it really depends on what you want to get out of the course. If you want to fully participate, you should plan on 4-8 hours/week. Readings and reflections could take anywhere from 1-4 (or even more) hours. You might end up discussing some of your projects, analyses, and creative work with other learners. And the activities are relatively open ended as well. It's possible to build a quick mix of a project in 30 minutes, or spend a full day refining it. Also, there is no requirement to complete everything.
